About the Project

A deep learning approach to identify plant diseases and provide treatment recommendations

Project Overview

This project implements a deep learning-based image classification system to detect and diagnose plant diseases using convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and other state-of-the-art models. By leveraging the PlantVillage dataset, the system is trained to classify various diseases affecting crops such as tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers.

The goal is to provide farmers and gardeners with a quick, accessible tool for early disease detection, enabling timely interventions and reducing crop losses. The system uses advanced image processing techniques to identify patterns associated with different plant diseases and provides actionable treatment recommendations.

Technical Approach

Dataset

The system uses the PlantVillage dataset, which contains thousands of labeled images of healthy and diseased plant leaves across various crop species.

Models

Multiple deep learning models were implemented and compared, including a custom CNN, ResNet50, MobileNetV2, and EfficientNetB0.

Evaluation

Models were evaluated using metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score to ensure reliable disease classification.

Visualization

Grad-CAM technique is used to generate heatmaps that highlight the regions of the image most influential to the model's prediction.
